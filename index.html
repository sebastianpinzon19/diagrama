User Guide

How Finality Warranty works

A step-by-step guide for every person who uses the platform — seller, warranty manager, and administrator. Read only the section that applies to your role.

Overview
What does this system do?
Three simple ideas that explain everything.
📋
A warranty claim is a request
When a customer's product breaks and is still under warranty, a seller submits a claim on their behalf. The claim contains the customer's info, the product details, and what went wrong.
🔍
A manager reviews and decides
A warranty manager looks at every claim, fills in technical details (part numbers, service order), and either approves or rejects it. If they haven't touched a claim in over 24 hours, the system automatically marks it as urgent.
📄
Approval = a PDF certificate
When a claim is approved, the system automatically generates a warranty certificate as a PDF and emails it to the customer. If rejected, an email is sent explaining why.

Every claim moves through these stages
A claim always starts at Pending and ends at either Completed or Rejected. It can never skip steps.

Step-by-step by role
What does each person actually do?
Click your role below to see exactly what you do, screen by screen.
🧑‍💼

Seller

You represent the customer. Your job is to open a warranty claim when a product fails, and then track what happens to it. You cannot approve or reject — that's the manager's job.

1
Log in
Go to the login page
Enter your email and password. The system will automatically send you to your seller dashboard. You cannot self-register — an admin must create your account first.
2
Dashboard
See all your claims at a glance
Your dashboard shows every claim you've submitted and its current status. You can see at a glance which ones are pending, being reviewed, approved, or rejected. Click any claim to see its full detail.
3
New Claim
Fill out the warranty claim form
Click "New Claim" and fill in: the customer's name and contact info, the product model and serial number, the date of purchase, and a clear description of what failed. You'll also need to sign the form digitally before submitting.
4
Submitted
The claim is now in the system
Once submitted, the claim gets a unique number (e.g. WC-0042) and its status becomes pending. The warranty manager receives an automatic notification. You don't need to call anyone.
5
Wait & Track
You'll be notified at every step
You'll receive notifications inside the app and by email whenever the claim status changes. You don't need to follow up manually.
What happens at the end?
✓ If approved
You'll get an email with the warranty certificate attached as a PDF. You can also download it directly from the claim detail page at any time. Send or print it for the customer.
✗ If rejected
You'll get an email explaining exactly why it was rejected. The reason is also visible inside the claim detail page so you can read it anytime.
🛡️

Warranty Manager

You are the decision-maker. You review every claim that sellers submit, fill in technical details, and decide whether to approve or reject. You must act within 24 hours or the claim becomes urgent.

1
Dashboard
Your queue is waiting for you
When you log in, your dashboard shows three separate lists: Pending (new, untouched claims), Urgent (claims that have been waiting too long — marked with 🔥), and Approved (history). Always start with Urgent.
2
Open a Claim
Click a claim to read all the details
You'll see everything the seller submitted: customer information, product info, description of the problem, and the digital signature. Read it carefully before doing anything.
3
Mark as Under Review
Tell the system you're working on it
Click "Mark as Under Review". This changes the status to under_review and lets the seller know you're looking at it. Do this before anything else.
4
Internal Fields
Fill in the technical information
This section is only visible to managers. You need to fill in: Failed part number, replacement part number, Service Order (SO) number, and the service date. This information goes on the final certificate.
5
Priority
Set how urgent this claim is
You can manually assign a priority level to any claim: Low Normal High Urgent. Changes take effect instantly, no page reload needed.
6
Decision
Approve or Reject
Now make your decision.
Your decision
✓ Approve
Click Approve. The system immediately generates a PDF warranty certificate and emails it to the customer automatically. The claim moves to Completed.
✗ Reject
You must type a reason before rejecting. Be specific — the seller and customer will read it. Once submitted, a rejection email is sent automatically.
⚡
The 24-hour rule
Every hour, the system automatically checks all pending and under-review claims. If a claim hasn't been touched in more than 24 hours, it is automatically flagged as Urgent and appears with a 🔥 badge at the top of your queue. Admins also receive an alert email. Act quickly to avoid escalations.
👑

Administrator

You have full visibility and control over everything. You don't process claims day-to-day — you manage the people who do, configure the system, and monitor overall performance.

1
First thing to do
Create user accounts for your team
No one can self-register. You must create every account. Go to Users → Create User, set their name, email, password, and role (Seller or Warranty Manager). You can also deactivate or delete accounts anytime.
2
Dashboard
See the health of the entire operation
Your global dashboard shows live numbers: total claims this month, how many are pending, the approval rate percentage, and average time to resolve. If numbers look off, drill into the claims list.
3
All Warranties
View and search every claim in the system
Unlike managers (who only see their queue), you can see every claim from every seller. Use filters by status, date, or seller name to find specific claims. You can also view the full detail of any claim.
4
Analytics
Understand trends over time
The analytics section shows monthly claim volume charts, approval vs. rejection rates, which sellers submit the most claims, and which products fail most often. Use this to identify patterns and report to management.
5
Settings
Configure email and notification rules
In Settings you can: set which email address receives overdue claim alerts, configure how many days before a claim is considered overdue, and manage notification preferences per user.
6
Audit Trail
Every action is logged — nothing is hidden
The system records every action any user takes: who submitted a claim, who approved it, who changed a status, and exactly when. If there's ever a dispute, the audit log is your source of truth.
Supporting systems
What happens behind the scenes
These systems run automatically — you don't need to do anything to trigger them.
📬
Automatic emails & notifications
Sent automatically at each stage
  • Claim submitted → Manager gets an in-app notification
  • Under review → Seller knows manager is working on it
  • Approved → Customer receives PDF certificate by email
  • Rejected → Seller receives rejection reason by email
  • Overdue (>24 h) → Admin receives an alert email
🔒
Security — what protects your data
Built-in, always on
  • Passwords are encrypted — even the system can't read them
  • Sessions expire — you're logged out automatically after 24 h
  • Role isolation — sellers can only see their own claims
  • Every action is logged — full audit trail, always
  • No public registration — only admins create accounts
